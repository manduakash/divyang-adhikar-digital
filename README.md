1. Citizens / Beneficiaries
Persons with Disabilities (PwDs): The primary beneficiaries who use the Public App to maintain a master profile, apply for disability certificates, lodge grievances, and request emergency disaster assistance.
Caregivers / Guardians: Support persons who interact with the system on behalf of PwDs, helping them manage profiles, submit applications, and track service requests.
2. Establishments & Institutions
Government & Private Establishments: Responsible for institutional compliance regarding physical/digital accessibility, equal opportunity policies, and mandatory PwD employment quotas.
Educational Institutions (Govt/Aided/Private): Monitored to ensure inclusive education, track PwD enrollment, provide accessible facilities, and grant reasonable accommodations to students.
Registered Institutions for PwDs (NGOs/Rehabs): Entities operating in the disability sector that use the system to validate their registration, document activities, and resolve facility-directed grievances.
3. District-Level Authorities
District Administration (DM / Collector): The nodal supervisory authority at the district level that inspects establishments, enforces the PwD Act, and issues corrective directions for non-compliance.
District Grievance Redressal Officer (GRO): The first institutional response layer responsible for acknowledging, addressing, and uploading Action Taken Reports (ATRs) for PwD complaints within strict timelines.
District Education Officer (DEO): Supervises inclusive education provisions and resolves education-specific grievances, ensuring schools provide physical accessibility and exam accommodations.
District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA): Manages local disaster preparedness by tagging high-risk PwDs, mapping accessible shelters, and coordinating emergency evacuation and medical transport.
District Special Public Prosecutor (District PP): Tracks legal proceedings and court cases related to the PwD Act, updating hearing dates, case stages, and compliance with court orders.
4. State-Level Authorities
State Nodal Department (PwD): Coordinates the overall state-wide implementation of the Act, managing inter-departmental coordination, monitoring district performance, and compiling state reports.
State Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities: Acts as the state’s quasi-judicial authority to review escalated grievances, monitor court cases, and issue formal compliance orders to establishments.
State Advisory Board on Disability: A policy-focused governance body that conducts scheduled meetings to formulate state-level welfare recommendations and track the execution of action points.
State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA): Provides strategic oversight of PwD-inclusive disaster readiness, issuing early warnings and managing state-wide resource allocation during emergencies.
Special Employment Exchange for PwDs: Monitors the submission of statutory six-monthly and annual employment returns to track PwD hiring percentages and the filling of identified vacancies.
5. Central-Level Authorities
Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment (DEPwD): The national executive authority that oversees statutory compliance, drafts national policy guidelines, and tracks parliamentary questions and assurances.
Chief Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities (CCPD): The highest national supervisory authority that handles nationwide grievances, monitors state-wise compliance, and publishes the annual implementation report.
National Advisory Board on Disability (NABD): The central advisory body responsible for formulating national policies, conducting high-level meetings, and tracking the implementation of national welfare recommendations.
National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA): Oversees India-wide PwD disaster risk profiles, sets national SOPs/guidelines for inclusive evacuations, and coordinates inter-state emergency responses.
National Disability Finance & Development Corporation (NDFDC): Oversees national financial assistance programs, monitoring the disbursement of funds and the performance of developmental schemes for PwDs.
6. System Administration
Super / State / District Administrators: Responsible for the technical governance of the platform, managing user roles, configuring alert thresholds, and auditing data security and API integrations.
